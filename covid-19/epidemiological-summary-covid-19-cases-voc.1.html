Variants in Canada

All viruses, including COVID-19, change or mutate over time. Not all mutations are of concern. However, some mutations result in variants of concern (VOC). A VOC has mutations that are significant to public health.

For example, they might:

  • spread more easily
  • cause more severe illness
  • require different treatments, or
  • not respond as well to current vaccines

The Public Health Agency of Canada (PHAC), in collaboration with Provincial and Territorial partners and CanCOGeN, sequences a percentage of all positive COVID-19 test results. PHAC reports the proportion of COVID-19 variants in Canada weekly.

Canada is collecting evidence to determine if new variants meet the definition for a VOC or a variant of interest (VOI). Many variants are being tracked across Canada and internationally. Most of these variants do not cause more severe illness. However, VOCs now represent a majority of COVID-19 cases in Canada.

Four VOCs have been detected in most provinces and territories:

  • B.1.1.7 (Alpha)
  • B.1.351 (Beta)
  • P.1 (Gamma)
  • B.1.617.2 (Delta)

In addition, there are several VOIsVOIs that have mutations similar to VOCs but without evidence yet of an elevated risk to public health. New variants will continue to appear. It is crucial to remain vigilant and take all available measures to limit spread.
